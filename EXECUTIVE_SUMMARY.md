# Monthly Business Review
## Banking Customer Intelligence Platform
### Portfolio Snapshot: January 2024

---

## What Happened

The portfolio generated **£1,711,764 in fee revenue** over the last 12 months across 1,000 customer relationships. Quarter-on-quarter, portfolio revenue declined by **3.4%** (£406,840 vs £421,020 in the prior quarter) — the first negative quarter recorded in this review cycle.

Customer quality deteriorated across the board. **265 customers (26.5%)** were reclassified to a worse segment since last quarter, compared to only 69 upgrades (6.9%). **140 customers (14.0%)** are now fully dormant, generating zero fee revenue in the last 90 days. The proportion of customers classified as Healthy stands at just **28.9%**, with the majority — **53.3%** — sitting in the Watchlist category, indicating early-stage health deterioration.

On the RM workbook, **652 open exceptions** were detected by the system, including **4 Priority 1 alerts** requiring senior RM attention within 24 hours. Of the 1,000 customers, **102 require a Retain action** (protecting revenue), **140 require Reactivation** (recovering dormant relationships), and **93 are cross-sell opportunities** for revenue growth.

---

## Why It Matters

The defining risk in this portfolio is **revenue concentration**. The top 10 customers — all VIP-tier, all Healthy — account for **£879,206 or 51.4% of all portfolio revenue**. The Revenue Dependency Score is **CRITICAL**, the highest severity rating. By comparison, customer ranked 11th contributes just £5,735 per year — a 13-fold drop from customer ranked 10th. Revenue does not taper off gradually after the top tier; it falls off a cliff.

This concentration creates asymmetric business risk. A single relationship exit in the top 10 removes, on average, **£87,921 in annual fee revenue** — equivalent to 51 Regular-tier customers combined. The portfolio has no natural buffer against VIP attrition.

The quarter-on-quarter revenue decline makes this risk live, not theoretical. Four of the 10 VIP clients — Evans LLC, Lee Marsden and Evans, Thomas and Sons, and Palmer Group — are already reporting **revenue declines of 22–29%** versus the prior quarter. These four accounts collectively represent £342,343 in LTM revenue. If their current trajectory continues, the portfolio's largest revenue-generating relationships are actively eroding.

---

## What Risk Exists

**Immediate (P1):** Four VIP clients with active revenue decline of 22–29% vs prior quarter. These are the portfolio's highest-revenue relationships, generating £76K–£94K each in LTM fee revenue. Combined revenue in current quarter: £73,682 (down from £97,915 in the prior quarter). Senior RM intervention is required within 24 hours.

**Structural:** Revenue dependency is CRITICAL. The top 10 clients contribute 51.4% of all revenue; the top 20 contribute 54.5%; the top 50 contribute 63.2%. This is not a long-tail distribution — it is a concentration pattern that exposes the portfolio to catastrophic revenue loss from a small number of relationship exits.

**Portfolio-wide:** 242 customers (24.2%) are on a deteriorating health trend. Of particular concern: **24 customers currently classified as Healthy are already deteriorating** — these are early warnings that are not yet visible in the segment or health category, but will manifest as Watchlist reclassifications next quarter if not addressed.

**Dormant backlog:** 140 customers have been inactive for 90+ days. Their cumulative LTM revenue contribution is under £2,000 — they have effectively stopped engaging with the bank. The reactivation window is closing.
